WHAT TO BRING

👟

Hiking Shoes

Proper hiking boots or trail shoes. No sandals, no city sneakers. These are real trails with rocks, mud, and roots.

💧

Water (1L+)

Minimum 1 liter, more in summer. I'll bring extra but you should have your own supply.

🧥

Layers

Weather changes fast in mountains. Light jacket, rain shell recommended.

🍫

Snacks

Energy bars, nuts, fruit. I'll bring some local snacks to share.

📱

Phone + Power Bank

For photos and GPS backup. China mobile signal is surprisingly good on most trails.

💊

Personal Meds

I have basic first aid, but bring your own medications if needed.

⚠️ SAFETY FIRST

These are wilderness trails, not tourist attractions. Weather can change, trails can be challenging, and cell service may be limited in some areas. I'm trained in first aid and know these trails well, but you need to be honest about your fitness level and any medical conditions before we start.

  • ✓ All trails are pre-scouted and conditions checked

  • ✓ Your safety is my #1 priority — we turn back if needed
